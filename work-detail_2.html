Hear

An Interactive Learning & Social App for the Musician Community

Design Proposal

We propose the creation of a mobile phone-based app
to address health information needs of musicians who are concerned about hearing health or who already suffer from hearing loss


How I do:

After listing out the main purposes we would like to achieve on this APP, my partner and I designed and organized it primarily into five functional sections: Learning, News, Social Networking, Social Support Groups, and Provider Location Services. Additionally, the app will include links to curated self-monitoring resources and self-care products.

Background

Why is auditory health important?

Unlike some tissues in our bodies, mammalian cochlear hair cells do not regenerate, either spontaneously or after damage.

According to Better Hearing Institute, most sensorineural hearing loss can't be reversed with medical treatment and is typically described as irreversible.



Hearing loss is not as simple as you think!

Hearing loss is also linked with dementia, and atrophy in brain, as highlighted in recent research from Johns Hopkins.

Hearing loss also contributes to social isolation which may result in dementia since you may not want to be with people or engage in conversation.



Vulnerable group of people

There is a group of people whose auditory health is at particularly high risk for damage: musicians.

However, even when these populations might perceive that their auditory health is in danger, few of them take steps to maintain their hearing health.
