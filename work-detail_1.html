Exploring Digital Newsroom

TxDOT x UT iSchool



By client kickoff meeting I learned that Texas Department of Transportation (TxDOT) wants to deliver the messages on its own will, instead of being controlled by local media. Also, they want to raise its brand awareness and bridge the communication gap between TxDOT and the public.


Problem Space:

TxDOT is having trouble relying on the local media to spread the brand message because the local media is not motivated to promote the positive spin TxDOT wants to apply. However, having no idea of what would take to open their own Digital/Corporate Newsroom.

How I do:

After sorting out several aspects of research questions using affinity diagram, I strategized several research methods to focus on different problems, including both generative and evaluative research. I performed early research about the elements of a good online newsroom to clarify the research problem. Then, I used subject matter expert to gain insights and conducted interview and surveys for average drivers to know how they plan for a trip and find out more about driver’s behaviors. I also executed user testing to figure out the pain points of the current website.

My Role:

Research, Subject matter expert (SME) interviews, Competitive Analysis, Survey, 1-1 interviews, Usability test, Quantitative data analysis

Tools:

Sketch, Qualtrics, Zoom, UserZoom

What I learn:

What I learned from this project is how to use different methods to solve problems. Also, using Sketch to compose a complete final report.








What I learned from Literature Review is

1. Social media can help to broaden the audience group.

2. Segment the audience group and personalized the message delivered toward audience group will help reach out more people.

3. Sharing stories of the users can build the affinity, increase interactivity and the opportunity to reach potential customers.




What I learned from Subject Matter Expert is

1. Facebook has a stable and big user population.

2. Young adults prefer to get news on mobile, while elders prefer computers.

3. Sharing by friends via social media is a powerful tool for spreading the words.




What I learned from Survey is

1. People are interested in receiving traffic conditions, constructions and travel information.

2. Website and mobile application is the most common way people look up for news, weather and traffic.




What I learned from Interview is

1. People is having a hard time getting information about construction, either don’t know where to find it or already known for a while.

2. People mostly use Google to check on road, however, they also use other local applications which could help them attain accurate information.

3. Older people have a better knowing of what TxDOT do, in contrast, younger generation doesn’t know what TxDOT do or have misunderstanding of it.




What I learned from Usability Testing is

1. Users are not sure whether if they complete the task or not.

2. People use search bar function a lot to find what they want.




By Competitive Analysis, I want to find out what elements or contents existing within a good newsroom.

Also, how they interact and communicate with users on social platforms in order to maintain its brand or even promoting it.
