Exploring Digital Newsroom

TxDOT x UT iSchool



By client kickoff meeting I learned that Texas Department of Transportation (TxDOT) wants to deliver the messages on its own will, instead of being controlled by local media. Also, they want to raise its brand awareness and bridge the communication gap between TxDOT and the public.


Probelm Space:

TxDOT is having trouble relying on the local media to spread the brand message because the local media is not motivated to promote the positive spin TxDOT wants to apply. However, having no idea of what would take to open their own Digital/Corporate Newsroom.






After listing out research questions, I use affinity diagram to classify the problem scope.






After sorting out several aspects of research questions, I strategized several research mothods to focus on different problems,

including Competitive Analysis, Subject Matter Expert (SME), Survey, Interview, and Usability Testing.




WHAT I WANT TO KNOW FROM LIETERATURE REVIEW IS

1. How people perceive information

2. What other ways to reach audience

3. What content is useful




WHAT I WANT TO KNOW FROM SUBJECT MATTER EXPERT IS

1. What are the relationship beween different platforms

2. What content preferences between different consumers




WHAT I WANT TO KNOW FROM SURVEY IS

1. What content should be provided

2. What device do consumers use to get information

3. Where do they find the information




WHAT I WANT TO KNOW FROM INTERVIEW IS

1. Who are the users

2. What are the driver's behaviors

3. How people perceive TxDOT




WHAT I WANT TO KNOW FROM USABILITY TESTING IS

1. How peoepl find information

2. How do they feel while looking up for information




BY COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS, I want to find out what elements or contents existing within a good newsroom, vice versa.

Also, how they interact and communicate with users on social platforms in order to maintain its brand or even promoting it.







I targeted the users referred from the persona built by past research, including CDL driver and normal drivers.

There are tow groups of people in normal drivers, which were normal drivers and those who had evcuation experiences.






In SURVEY, I collapsed the results from 331 people, who are mostly from Texas and over half of them live in the city.






In INTERVIEW, I conducted INTERVIEW and USABILITY TESTING at the same session within 50 minutes from 11 people.




My 1st recommendation


People are having a hard time getting information about road safety. Create an app using Google Maps toolkit, users will not only already known how to use it,

but also provide future construction. You can reframe yourself of being a cool government agency by engaging technology.




My 2nd recommendation


Young people normally don’t know well about TxDOT. To attract them, you should create content for scenic route in your app and make sure to include gas station and rest

stop which are the common things people will be looking on while planning a trip. Also, young people like to go to National park for roadtrip that has no signal while traveling,

thus, adding offline function map in the app would be useful.




My 3rd recommendation


You have to meet users where they are. Make sure everything is optimized for Mobile becuase we’ve already known that’s where users are.

Utilize social media as the main delivery channel, not the website. Add the sharing function to reach out to more audience.

If you want people to go back from social media to the website, then redesign it.



My 4th recommendation


As I said, social media is a great way to promote the app and TxDOT itself. Having 3-4 posts per week on facebook, pictures or videos are the best.

Finally, provide a comment section for people to express themselves can make them feel related to this brand.
