Accessibility Research

Express Scripts



As UX researchers, we should care not only about the majority but about everyone.

In 2019 summer, I worked as an accessibility research intern in Express Scripts. Develop accessible website is not just about violating the laws or the matter of being disabled people. You and I, as a person will also be disabled at some point of your life. For example, people with cognitive disabilities, people recovered from concussion and distracted drivers. For these three kinds of people, they all have to complete tasks with limited attention. It is a mind blowing experience of observing how participants navigate websites with assistive technology and it really confirmed to me the importance of inclusive design.


Problem Space:
Users are not able to navigate the website using keyboard only which could be problematic for screen reader users or people who only have keyboard at hand. In addition, color contrast and font size are also another issues for low vision users or people viewing the website under poor lighting. Creating content that make sense even out of context is essential since it would be hard for screen reader users to understand what's the difference between all the "Read more" links as well as for people with cognitive disabilities.

How I do:
At early stage of the website development, I performed heuristic evaluation for digital experiences and classified accessibility issues into different severity in terms of WCAG violation and task failure. Then, me and my colleagues co-facilitated accessibility user testing with screen reader users who are blind to gain feedback on digital products. Furthermore, I conducted manual and automated accessibility testing to inform design principles.

My Role:
Heuristic Evaluation, 1-1 interviews, Usability Testing

Tools:
JAWS, JAWS Inspect, Color checker, WCAG, ARC toolkit, Nu html checker, Accessibility Insights for Web, Sketch

What I learn:
During that time, I learned from understanding what is accessibility, related regulations and laws in terms of accessbility, to practices of conducting manual and automated testing with assistive technology of using a variety of tools to promote inclusive design.
Besides technical skills, I also developed some soft skills. For example, everything is in timely manner and volatile when working in the real world. You have to distribute time and work properly and plan ahead.
In regards to communication skills, you have to understand the "language" between sectors in company as well as goals and priorities for each team.
