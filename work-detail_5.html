Accessibility Research

Express Scripts



As UX researchers, we should care not only about the majority but about everyone.

In 2019 summer, I worked as an accessibility research intern. Develop accessible website is not only about violating the or the matter of being disabled people. You and I, as a person will also be disabled at some point of your life. For example, you may not be able to navigate the webiste, that is situational disabled. It is a mind blowing experience of observing how participants navigate websites with assistive technology and it really confirmed to me the importance of inclusive design.


Problem Space:
The website cannot be navigated through keyboard only easily. In addition, low color contrast would be. Inconsistent

How I do:
At early stage of the website development, I performed heuristic evaluation for digital experiences and classified accessibility issues into different severity in terms of WCAG violation and task failure. Then, me and my colleagues co-facilitated accessibility user testing with screen reader users who are blind to gain feedback on digital products. At last, I analyzed data from the sessions, developed report with enhancement recommendations.

My Role:
Heuristic Evaluation, 1-1 interviews, Usability Testing

Tools:
JAWS, JAWS Inspect, Color checker, WCAG, Sketch

What I learn:
During that time, I learned from understanding what is accessibility, related regulations and laws in terms of accessbility, to practices of conducting munual and automated testing with assistive technology of using a variety of tools to promote inclusive design..
Besides technical skills, I also devloped some sofe skills. For example, everything is in timely manner and volatile when working in the real world. You have to distribute time and work properly and plan ahead.
In regerds to communication skills, you have to understand the "language" between sectors in company as well as goals and principles for each team.
