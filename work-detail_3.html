Electronic Decoding Diagnostic Inventory (EDDI)

UT College of Education



As UX researchers, we should care not only about the majority but about everyone.

In this project, I’m working with Dr. Nathan Clemens in the Department of Special Education to design a diagnostic assessment of individual students’ difficulties in reading words. Specifically, we are developing software that will provide in-depth diagnostic data on an individual students’ skills in reading words, including the letter sounds, word types, and spelling patterns a student reads incorrectly.


Problem Space:
The assessment will not be used as a screening tool to identify what students are struggling; rather, the program will intended for students who have already been identified with reading difficulties. The information from the assessment could be used by a teacher better understanding for designing instruction that more specifically targets a student’s reading needs.

How I do:
After sorting out several aspects of research questions using affinity diagram, I strategized several research methods to focus on different problems, including both generative and evaluative research. I performed early research about the elements of a good online newsroom to clarify the research problem. Then, I used subject matter expert to gain insights and conducted interview and surveys for average drivers to know how they plan for a trip and find out more about driver’s behaviors. I also executed user testing to figure out the pain points of the current website.

My Role:
Research, Subject matter expert (SME) interviews, 1-1 interviews, Qualitative data analysis

Tools:
Sketch, Figma

What I learn:
What I learned from this project is how to use in-depth qualitative data to inform our design and reiterate the product.
